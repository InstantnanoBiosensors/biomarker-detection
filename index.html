The Challenge: Detecting What Conventional Assays Miss

Clinical challenge: pathology precedes symptoms

Alzheimer's pathology develops years before the first cognitive symptoms appear. Blood-based biomarkers could make earlier and broader testing possible, but the most disease-relevant targets are present at extremely low concentrations, far below what standard immunoassays can reliably reach.

Scientific obstacle: Low detectability of pTau217

pTau217 is a disease-relevant phosphorylated tau species, defined by phosphorylation at threonine 217. Yet in blood, it represents only a minute fraction of total circulating tau. This extreme scarcity, often reaching sub-picogram-per-milliliter levels, makes detection highly vulnerable to background interference and matrix effects.

Practical Consequence

Without sufficient sensitivity and robust assay design, clinically meaningful signals can be missed or confused with biological noise, delaying diagnosis and reducing diagnostic accuracy.

Stage 2 — Ultra-Sensitive Detection (FONLISA)

GOAL: Achieve femtomolar/attomolar detection

The FOPPR-selected capture antibody is immobilized on the surface of an uncladded glass fiber. When the target biomarker, such as pTau217, is present, it first binds to the capture antibody and then recruits an AuNP-conjugated detection antibody, forming a sandwich complex on the fiber surface.

Key Mechanism

As light propagates through the fiber by multiple total internal reflection, an evanescent field is generated at the fiber–medium interface. Because no gold nanoparticles (AuNPs) are initially present within this sensing region, the guided light experiences negligible nanoparticle-induced absorption. As a result, the baseline transmission is essentially 100%, corresponding to a zero-absorbance background. When target-bound gold nanoparticles enter this confined optical field, they interact strongly with the guided light through particle plasmon resonance. This interaction results in a measurable drop in transmitted light intensity at the fiber output, which is proportional to the analyte concentration.

The FOPPR advantage over conventional SPR for antibody screening

Conventional SPR sensor chips use a flat 2D gold surface, often modified with a dextran matrix to increase binding capacity. While this helps create a more 3D-like surface, SPR assays still require careful optimization of surface chemistry, ligand density, flow rate, and regeneration conditions to minimize mass-transfer limitations, rebinding artifacts, and ligand damage between regenration cycles. FOPPR uses a glass-fiber sensor coated with gold nanoparticles to create a more accessible 3D binding environment. This design allows association and dissociation to occur with less surface restriction, supports more representative kinetic measurements, and eliminates regeneration between screening steps. The result is a faster, simpler workflow with a lower technical barrier and minimal training.

FOPPR performs real-time, label-free binding analysis, affinity ranking, and epitope binning in a single run evaluating up to four antibodies simultaneously. No regeneration step is required. As a result, screening is faster and ligand damage is avoided.
Why is pTau217 harder to detect than total tau?
pTau217 represents only a small fraction of total tau in plasma. This low relative abundance, combined with background interference and matrix effects means only ultra-sensitive platforms with very low background noise can reliably quantify it at clinically meaningful concentrations.
How does FONLISA compare to ELISA and SIMOA for for low-abundance biomarker detection?
Conventional ELISA often lacks the sensitivity needed to quantify ultra-low-abundance biomarkers in complex samples such as plasma. ELISA and SIMOA are typically endpoint assays, which provide a final signal after incubation but may miss fast-dissociating binding interactions. FONLISA measures binding in real time, captures kinetic information such as association and dissociation behavior, and achieves femtomolar-to-attomolar sensitivity in a compact analytical platform.
How to reduce background noise in plasma-based pTau217 assays?
FONLISA's sandwich design generates the AuNP signal only when the target biomarker is captured between both antibodies. This dual-recognition requirement keeps background noise very low even in complex plasma matrices, supporting reliable sub-femtomolar quantification.
How can ultra-sensitive Alzheimer’s biomarker measurement become more accessible beyond centralized laboratories?
FONLISA is a compact analyzer using glass fiber, nano particles and microfluidics with no external tubing. Its small footprint makes it suitable for decentralized settings, enabling ultra-sensitive plasma biomarker testing beyond large academic medical centers.
